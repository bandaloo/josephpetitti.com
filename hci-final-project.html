Design Manifesto

Human Computer Interaction

Human Computer Interaction (HCI) is the intersection of computer science and design. It uses aspects of art, science, and engineering to build computer systems that are powerful, intuitive, and pleasing to use. In CS 3041, I explored the basics of HCI through six design sprints that included everything from accessibility for the elderly to virtual reality game design.

The class has helped me learn a lot about HCI in particular, but also about the design process in general. Each successive design sprint gave fewer specifics and more freedom, allowing gradually guiding students to find what design steps work best for them. I these main points to be the most important across each project:

1. Brainstorming for the right solution

In Don Norman's "The Design of Everyday Things," the the first phase of the "Double-Diamond" model of design is actually finding the right problem. For the design sprints in CS 3041, we were usually given a clear problem to solve, allowing us to often skip this step. I found this first step of the design process to often be the most difficult.
